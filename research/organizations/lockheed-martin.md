# Lockheed Martin

## Summary
Lockheed Martin is a global, security-focused aerospace and defense technology company. Formed in 1995 through the merger of Lockheed Corporation and Martin Marietta, the company provides integrated solutions across all warfighting domains, including aeronautics, missiles and fire control, rotary and mission systems, and space. It is a cornerstone of the American defense industry, known for its advanced technology systems and significant role in national security and scientific exploration.

## Detailed Findings

### Company Overview
- **Headquarters:** North Bethesda, Maryland, USA.
- **Business Areas:**
    - **Aeronautics:** Focuses on aircraft, flight systems, and related technologies (approx. 39% of 2024 revenues).
    - **Missiles and Fire Control:** Specialized in precision weapons and control systems (approx. 18% of 2024 revenues).
    - **Rotary and Mission Systems:** Covers advanced mission capabilities across various platforms (approx. 24% of 20． revenues).
    - **Space:** Provides solutions for orbital and deep space exploration and defense.
- **Core Competencies:** Research, design, development, manufacture, integration, and sustainment of advanced technology systems. This includes cybersecurity, intelligence, homeland security, and information technology.

### Historical Context
- **Merger:** The modern entity was created on March 15, 1995, through the merger of the Lockheed Corporation and Martin Marietta.
- **Foundations:** The company's roots trace back to the early 20th century, including the Glenn L. Martin Company (established 1912) and the Alco Hydro-Aeroplane Company (later Lockheed).

### Innovation and Skunk Works
- **Skunk Works:** One of Lockheed Martin's most famous divisions, Skunk Works, is an elite team of engineers and mechanics. Known for its "fail fast" approach to innovation, it has been responsible for many of the most advanced and secretive aerospace projects, including stealth technology and advanced prototype aircraft.
- **Legacy of Innovation:** Since its inception in 1943 under Chief Research Engineer Kelly Johnson, Skunk Works has been at the forefront of pushing the boundaries of aerospace technology.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Primary Business Sectors:** Defense, Space, Intelligence, Homeland Security.
- **Key Division:** Skunk Works.
- **Founding Figures (Historical):** Kelly Johnson (Skunk Works founder), Glenn L. Martin, Allan and Malcolm Lockheed.
- **Key Merger Date:** March 15, 1995.
