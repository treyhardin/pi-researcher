# Martin Marietta

## Summary
The Martin Marietta Corporation was a prominent American aerospace and defense company. Formed in 1961 through the merger of the Glenn L. Martin Company and the American-Marietta Corporation, it became a major player in the defense industry. In 1995, it merged with the Lockheed Corporation to create the modern-day Lockheed Martin Corporation.

## Detailed Findings
The Martin Marietta Corporation's history is defined by its consolidation of aerospace and manufacturing capabilities. 

### Formation
The company was established in 1961 following the merger of:
- **Glenn L. Martin Company**: A well-known aircraft manufacturer.
- **American-Marietta Corporation**: A manufacturing entity.

### The Lockheed Merger
In August 1994, Martin Marietta and Lockheed Corporation announced a definitive agreement for a "merger of equals." The plan was to reorganize as separate subsidiaries of a new holding company, which would be named Lockheed Martin Corporation. This massive merger, finalized in 1995, combined the strengths of both companies in the aerospace, defense, and security sectors.

### Industry Presence
Before its merger, Martin Marietta was a significant contributor to U.S. aerospace and defense capabilities, participating in various high-profile government and military projects.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Founded:** 1961
- **Merged with Lockheed:** 1995
- **Predecessors:** Glenn L. Martin Company, American-Marietta Corporation
- **Successor:** Lockheed Martin
