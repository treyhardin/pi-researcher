# Raytheon (RTX)

## Summary
Raytheon (an RTX business) is a preeminent global aerospace and defense company that plays a pivotal role in the development of advanced sensing, radar, and electronic warfare (EW) technologies. Raytheon is significantly connected to the study and detection of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) due to its production of highly sensitive radar systems (such as the AN/APG-79 AESA) and advanced infrared sensors (such as the Advanced Targeting FLIR) which have been used by U.S. Navy pilots to detect and record anomalous aerial objects during notable encounters, including the 2004 Nimitz incident.

## Detailed Findings

### Advanced Sensing and Radar Technology
Raytheon's technological contributions are central to modern aerial surveillance and the detection of anomalous objects.
* **AESA Radar (AN/APG-79):** The transition from mechanical radar to the Raytheon-produced **Active Electronically Scanned Array (AESA)** radar on F/A-18 Super Hornets is a critical factor in recent UAP detections. This radar provided a substantial leap in detection sensitivity, allowing for the identification of contacts that previous systems missed.
* **Advanced Targeting FLIR:** During the 2004 Nimitz encounter, a **Raytheon Advanced Targeting Forward Look Infrared (FLIR) sensor** was instrumental in capturing video evidence of the phenomenon.
* **Intelligent-Sensing (RAIVEN):** Raytheon is developing **RAIVEN**, a revolutionary electro-optical intelligent-sensing capability. RAIVEN enables simultaneous optical and spectral identification in real-time, significantly enhancing threat identification and tracking capabilities.
* **AI/ML-Powered Radar Warning Receivers (RWR):** Raytheon has successfully flight-tested the first-ever AI/ML-powered RWR for 4th-generation aircraft, integrating deep learning to enhance situational awareness.

### Electronic Warfare (EW) and Electronic Attack (EA)
Raytheon is a leader in managing the "invisible battlespace" through advanced electronic warfare capabilities.
* **Advanced Electronic Warfare (ADWEV) Prototype:** Raytheon is developing the **ADWEV** prototype for the U.S. Navy's F/A-18E/F Super Hornet to replace legacy systems and enhance aircraft survivability.
* **Electronic Attack (EA) Capabilities:** The company focuses on developing airborne electronic attack technologies to manipulate the electromagnetic spectrum, enabling aircraft to operate effectively in contested environments.

### Strategic Role in the Defense Industry
* **Part of RTX:** Raytheon operates as a key business unit within **RTX (formerly Raytheon Technologies)**, working in tandem with other major aerospace entities like Lockheed Martin.
* **Defense Contractor for U.S. Navy and Air Force:** Raytheon is a primary provider of mission-critical systems (radar, sensors, EW) for the most advanced U.S. military platforms, including the F-18, F-35, and various naval combat systems.

## Key Entities/Facts
* **Primary Business Unit:** Raytheon (an RTX business).
* **Key Technologies:** AN/APG-79 AESA Radar, Advanced Targeting FLIR, RAIVEN (Intelligent-Sensing), AI/ML-powered RWR, ADWEV.
* **Core Contribution to UAP Research:** Provision of the high-sensitivity sensor suites (radar and infrared) used by military pilots for the detection and recording of UAP.
* **Primary Sector:** Aerospace, Defense, Electronic Warfare, Advanced Sensing.
