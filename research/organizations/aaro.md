# All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO)

## Summary
The All-domain Anomaly Resolutionized Office (AARO) is the United States government's lead office for addressing Unidentified Anomally Phenomena (UAP). AARO uses a rigorous scientific framework and a data-driven approach to investigate, analyze, and resolve reports of unidentified airborne, transmedium, and submerged objects.

## Detailed Findings
AARO is part of the Department of Defense (DoD) and works closely with the Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI). Its authority and responsibilities were significantly expanded by the Fiscal Year 2023 National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA).

Key aspects of AARO's work include:
- **Annual Reporting:** AARO is required to provide annual, joint reports to both the DoD and ODNI, covering UAP incidents and findings.
- **Case Resolution:** The office publishes "UAP Case Resolution Reports," which analyze specific incidents (e.g., the Al Taqaddum Air Base event) to determine if they exhibit anomalous behavior or represent known technology/phenomena.
- **Data Collection:** AARO reviews reports from military and intelligence sensors, analyzing data to identify patterns, trends, and potential threats.
- **Scientific Approach:** The office emphasizes a scientific, evidence-based methodology to move beyond speculation and provide actionable intelligence.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Primary Mission:** Leading the U.S. government's efforts to address and resolve UAP reports.
- **Key Personnel:** 
    - **Sean Kirkpatrick, Ph.D.:** Former Director of AARO.
    	- **Dr. Jon Kosloski:** Provided testimony to the Senate Armed Services Committee regarding AARO and UAP.
- **Significant Incident:** **Al Taqaddum Air Base, Iraq (2017):** An incident where an infrared sensor recorded an unidentified object appearing to float above the ground, which AARO assessed with high confidence as not exhibiting anomalous behavior.
- **Regulatory Framework:** The FY23 NDAA expanded AARO's mission, structure, and reporting requirements.
