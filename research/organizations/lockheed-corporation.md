# Lockheed Corporation

## Summary
The Lockheed Corporation was a prominent American aerospace manufacturer, founded in 1926. In 1995, it merged with Martin Marietta Corporation to form the global defense and aerospace giant, Lockheed Martin. The company has a significant history in the development of advanced aircraft, spacecraft, and defense systems.

## Detailed Findings
The Lockheed Corporation's roots trace back to the early 20th century, with its founder, Allan Lockheed, having previously been involved in the Alco Hydro-Aeroplane Company (later renamed the Lockheed Aircraft Company). The company played a pivotal role in the evolution of modern aviation and space flight.

### Key Milestones
- **1926**: Official founding of the Lockheed Corporation.
- **1995**: The landmark merger with Martin Marietta Corporation, creating Lockheed Martin, which established the company as one of the world's largest defense contractors.
- **Expansion**: Through various acquisitions, the company expanded its capabilities in missiles, space systems, and advanced aeronautics.

### Industry Impact
Lockheed Corporation was instrumental in the development of various critical aerospace technologies, including reconnaissance aircraft and even early space flight's foundational components. Their work spanned across aeronautics, missile defense, and mission systems.

### Connections to UAP/UFO Research

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Allan Lockheed**: Founder of the company.
- **Martin Marietta**: The partner in the 1995 merger that formed Lockheed Martin.
- **Aerospace and Defense**: The primary industry of the corporation.
- **Lockheed Martin**: The successor corporation resulting from the merger.
