# Denison University

## Summary
Denison University is a private liberal arts college in Granville, Ohio. In the context of unconventional physics and propulsion research, it is historically significant as the location where Thomas Townsend Brown conducted early experiments that led to the discovery of the Biefeld-Brown effect, under the mentorship of Dr. Paul Alfred Biefeld.

## Detailed Findings
Denison University's historical connection to the study of electrogravitics and the Biefeld-Brown effect is deeply rooted in its relationship with the researchers involved.

### Connection to Thomas Townsend Brown
- **Early Education:** Before his time at Caltech, Thomas Townsend Brown attended Doane Academy, a preparatory school associated with Denison University, from 1921 to 1923.
- **Experimental Laboratory:** During his time at Denison (1924–1925), Brown was able to pursue his interest in the link between electricity and gravity. He found a "sympathetic ear" and mentorship in Dr. Paul Alfred Biefeld.
- **The Discovery Context:** It was through these early investigations at Denison, supported by Dr. Biefeld, that Brown began the experiments with high-voltage electrical charges that would eventually lead to the identification of the propulsion-related phenomenon now known as the Biefeld-Brown effect.

### Connection to Dr. Paul Alfred Biefeld
- **Leadership:** Dr. Paul Alfred Biefeld, the co-discoverer of the Biefeld-Brown effect, served as the Director of the Swazey Observatory at Denison University starting in 1911.
- **Mentorship:** His presence at Denison provided the academic and experimental environment necessary for young Brown to conduct the foundational research that would later shape modern discussions on advanced propulsion and electrohydrodynamics.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Dr. Paul Alfred Biefeld:** Director of the Swazey Observatory and mentor to T. Townsend Brown at Denison.
- **Thomas Townsend Brown:** Conducted pivotal early experiments at Denison University.
- **Swazey Observatory:** The astronomical facility at Denison where Biefeld served as Director.
- **Biefeld-Brown Effect:** The phenomenon discovered/investigated during this period.
