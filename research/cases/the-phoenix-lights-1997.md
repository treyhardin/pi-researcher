# The Phoenix Lights (1997)

## Summary
The "Phoenix Lights" refers to a massive, highly documented mass sighting of Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP) that occurred over Arizona, Nevada, and Mexico on the night of March 13, 1997. The event is characterized by two distinct phases: a large, silent, V-shaped craft moving across the sky, and a second event involving a row of bright, hovering lights. The scale of the sighting, involving an estimated 5,000 to 10,000 witnesses, makes it one of the most significant mass UAP encounters in history.

## Detailed Findings

### The Two Phases of the Event
The sightings occurred in two primary stages between 6:30 PM and 10:30 PM MST.

#### Phase 1: The V-Shaped Craft (The "Main Event")
- **Timeline:** Began around 6:55 PM in Henderson, Nevada, and moved south through Arizona.
- **Description:** A massive, "city-sized" V-shaped or boomerang-shaped object. Witnesses described it as a dark, solid, or "shadowy" structure, sometimes appearing to block out the stars.
- **Movement:** The craft traveled from Prescott/Prescott Valley (approx. 8:15 PM) through the Phoenix metropolitan area (approx. 8:30 PM) and was last seen heading toward the Mexican border (approx. 9:00 PM).
- **Acoustics:** A defining characteristic was the near-total silence of the craft, despite its enormous size and low altitude.

#### Phase 2: The Stationary Lights
- **Timeline:** Occurred later in the night, around 10:00 PM.
- **Description:** A line of bright, hovering lights appeared behind the Sierra Estrella mountain range southwest of Phoenix.
- **Nature:** These lights were much more stationary and were captured on numerous home video recordings.

### Official Explanations and Controversies
The official response to these sightings has been a subject of intense debate and skepticism.

- **The Flare Theory (Phase 2):** The U.S. Air Force officially stated that the 10:00 PM lights were LUU-2B/B illumination flares dropped by A-10 Warthog aircraft during a training exercise. While this may explain the second phase, it does not account for the massive, silent V-shaped craft observed two hours earlier.
- **Governor Fife Symington's Role:** Initially, Arizona Governor Fife Symington ridiculed the event, even holding a press conference with a staffer in an alien costume. However, he later recanted his skepticism, admitting that he had personally witnessed the craft and believed it was "otherworldly."

### Technical Anomalies
The reported characteristics of the V-shaped craft challenge conventional aviation:
- **Size:** Estimated at 1–2 miles wide, far larger than any known aircraft (e.append as "city-sized").
- **Acoustics:** Complete silence, contrary to the massive engine noise expected from a craft of that scale.
- **Propulsion/Speed:** Witnesses reported the object moving at extremely low, almost "walking" speeds, yet it traversed significant distances (from Prescott to the Mexican border) in a relatively short timeframe.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Region:** Arizona (Phoenix, Prescott, Tucson), Nevada, and Mexico.
- **Date:** March 13, 1997.
- **Witness Count:** Estimated between 5,000 and 10,000 people.
- **Key Figure:** Governor Fife Symington (Arizona).
- **Primary Shape:** V-shaped, boomerang, or "carpenter's square."
