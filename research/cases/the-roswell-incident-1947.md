# The Roswell Incident (1947)

## Summary
The Roswell incident refers to the recovery of unidentified debris near Roswell, New Mexico, in July 1947. While initially reported by the U.S. Army Air Forces as the recovery of a "flying disc," it later became the foundational event for modern UFO conspiracy theories, with claims ranging from the crash of an extraterrestrial spacecraft to the recovery of alien bodies. The U.S. military eventually clarified that the debris was part of a top-secret project involving high-altitude balloons.

## Detailed Findings
The Roswell incident is a complex historical event characterized by conflicting reports, government retractions, and decades of intense investigation.

### The Initial Event (July 1947)
- **The Discovery:** In early July 1947, debris was found on a ranch near Roswell, New Mexico.
- **The "Flying Disc" Announcement:** The Roswell Army Air Field (RAAF) issued a pre-release stating they had captured a "flying disc." This announcement caused a massive media sensation.
- **The Retraction:** Shortly after, the military changed its official stance, stating that the object was actually a weather balloon carrying a radar target (part of Project Mogul).

### Major Theories and Claims
- **Extraterrestrial Crash:** The most famous theory suggests that an alien spacecraft crashed near Roswell, and that the U.S. military recovered both wreckage and biological entities (alien bodies).
- **Project Mogul (The Official Explanation):** The official government explanation is that the object was a high-altitude balloon used to detect Soviet nuclear tests as part of the secret Project Mogul.
- **Cold War Espionage:** Some theories suggest the "UFO" was actually a secret surveillance device intended to monitor Soviet atmospheric nuclear testing.
- **The "Alien Bodies" Allegations:** Numerous accounts, including some referenced in recent National Archives video releases, claim that biological remains were recovered alongside the debris.

### Historical Significance
- **Birth of Modern UFO Lore:** Roswell is widely considered the "ground zero" of modern UFO culture, shifting the focus from mere sightings to the idea of physical evidence and government cover-ups.
- **Impact on Public Trust:** The initial discrepancy between the "flying disc" announcement and the subsequent "weather balloon" explanation fueled long-standing distrust in official government narratives.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Roswell Army Air Field (RAAF):** The military installation responsible for the initial report and recovery.
- **Project Mogul:** The top-int secret project involving high-altitude balloons that the military later identified as the true object.
- **Maj. Jesse A. Marcel**: The intelligence officer who investigated the site and was central to the early reports.
- **July 1947:** The timeframe of the incident and the initial press releases.
