# The Battle of Los Angeles (1942)

## Overview
The "Battle of Los Angeles," also known as the **Great Los Angeles Air Raid**, occurred during the night of **February 24–25, 1942**, shortly after the United States entered World War II. It involved a massive barrage of anti-aircraft fire by U.S. Army batteries against an unidentified airborne object(s) over the city of Los Angeles.

## Timeline and Events
- **Context:** The incident took place less than three months after the attack on Pearl Harbor. The West Coast was on high alert for potential Japanese aerial incursions.
- **The Incident:**
    - On the night of February 24, naval intelligence warnings and reports of flares/lights near defense plants caused heightened tension.
    - In the early hours of February 25, military radar tracked an object approximately 120 miles west of Los Angeles.
    - A citywide blackout was implemented.
    - Searchlights were deployed, and anti-aircraft batteries began firing.
- **The Barrage:** Approximately **1,430 rounds** of anti-aircraft ammunition were discharged over the course of the event.
- **Observations:** Observers reported seeing a glowing, moving object in the sky that appeared unaffected by the intense artillery fire. The object was illuminated by searchlights for over an hour.

## Conflicting Official Explanations
The lack of a clear explanation from the military command led to significant confusion:
- **U.S. Navy:** Attributed the event to "war nerves" among the population and garrisoned troops.
- **U.S. War Department:** Stated that a physical object was indeed present and tracked by radar, contradicting the "mass hysteria" narrative.
- **No Evidence of Enemy Aircraft:** Despite the massive barrage, no enemy aircraft were confirmed, and no wreckage was recovered from the area.

## UAP/UFO Significance
The Battle of Los Angeles is a cornerstone case in the study of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP). It is frequently cited due to:
- **Radar Verification:** The involvement of military radar tracking.
- **Non-Newtonian Behavior:** The reported ability of the object to maneuver and withstand heavy bombardment without being downed.
- **Historical Continuity:** It represents an early, large-scale, documented encounter in the modern era of aerial phenomena research.
