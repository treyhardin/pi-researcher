# Pascagoula Incident (1973)

## Summary
The "Pascagoula incident" refers to a highly documented and famous alleged UFO encounter and abduction that took place on October 11, 1973, near Pascagoula, Mississippi. Two shipyard workers, Charles Hickson and Calvin Parker, claimed they were intercepted by an egg-shaped, glowing craft and subsequently abducted by humanoid, silvery-skinned beings. The incident gained national fame due to a covert police recording of the witnesses' initial, distressed statements—often referred to as the "Hickson Tapes"—and the subsequent media frenzy. It remains a cornerstone of modern alien abduction narratives.

## Detailed Findings

### Timeline and Location
* **Date:** October 11, 1973 (late evening).
* **Location:** An abandoned fishing pier on the Pascagoula River, near Pascagoula, Mississippi.
* **Context:** Charles Hickson (42) and Calvin Parker (19) were fishing by the river when the encounter occurred.

### The Phenomenon and Encounter Details
The encounter involved an unidentified aerial phenomenon (UAP) and a close encounter with non-human entities.
* **The Craft:** Witnesses described a large, egg-shaped object (approximately 10 feet wide and 8 feet high) that was glowing and emitted a buzzing noise. It featured blue lights at the front.
* **The Entities:** The witnesses reported being approached by humanoid beings with silvery skin, large eyes, and pointed ears.
* **The Abduction/Examination:** The men claimed they were immobilized by the craft, taken aboard, and subjected to physical examinations/probes by the entities before being returned to the riverbank.
* **Physical Evidence Claims:** There were reports of physical trauma, such as puncture wounds, and the men were taken to a military hospital to be checked for potential radiation exposure.

### Key Witnesses and Witnesses' Accounts
* **Charles Hickson:** One of the primary witnesses; his later lifetime of interviews and his involvement in the original statements provided much of the case's narrative depth.
* **Calvin Parker:** The second primary witness; his account complemented Hickson's, detailing the terror and the physical sensation of the encounter.
* **The "Hickson Tapes":** A crucial, albeit controversial, element of the case. This is a covertly recorded conversation by local authorities (including Sheriff Fred Diamond) where the witnesses expressed extreme distress and fear, which many believe added credibility to their story.

### Investigations and Aftermath
* **Law Enforcement Involvement:** Local officers, including Sheriff Fred Diamond and Detective Tom Hunt 	Huntley, were central to the initial documentation of the event.
* **Media Frenzy:** The incident became a national sensation, covered by major news agencies like UPI and causing significant public interest and debate.
* **Scientific/Ufological Interest:** Prominent UFO researchers, including J. Allen Hynek, investigated the case. The case is often studied for its influence on the "abduction" archetype in popular culture.

## Key Entities/Facts
* **Primary Witnesses:** Charles Hickson, Calvin Parker.
* **Primary Vessel/Craft:** Egg-shaped, glowing, blue-lighted object.
* **Associated Program/Evidence:** The "Hickson Tapes" (police recordings).
* **Core Phenomenon:** Alien Abduction / Close Encounter of the Third Kind.
