# Tic Tac UCAV/UAP (2004 Nimitz Encounter)

## Summary
The "Tic Tac" incident refers to a series of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) encounters in November 2004 involving the U.S. Navy's Carrier Strike Group 11, specifically the USS Nimitz and the USS Princeton. The encounter is characterized by the observation of smooth, white, pill-shaped objects (resembling a Tic Tac) exhibiting extreme maneuverability, high speeds, and transmedium capabilities (moving from the ocean to the air) without visible propulsion or control surfaces.

## Detailed Findings

### The Encounter
In November 2004, while operating off the coast of Southern California, the U.S. Navy's Strike Carrier Group 11 (CSG-11) encountered multiple Anomalous Aerial Vehicles (AAVs). The encounters involved several F/A-18 Super Hornets and were tracked by advanced radar systems on the USS Princeton.

### Physical Description of the UAP
Witnesses, including Commander David Fravor, described the objects as:
- **Shape:** Smooth, white, elongated, similar to a "Tic Tac" candy.
- **Surface:** No visible flight control surfaces such as wings, rotors, or flaps.
- **Propulsion:** No visible exhaust, engine, or rotor wash.
- **Transmedium Capability:** Observations indicated the objects could emerge from the surface of the ocean and transition into flight (transmedium) seamlessly.

### Flight Characteristics and Maneuverability
The objects demonstrated "extreme" or "highly anomalous" flight characteristics:
- **Acceleration:** Capable of rapid, near-instantaneous acceleration and deceleration.
- **Altitude Changes:** Radar data showed objects descending from approximately 60,000 feet to sea level (as low as 50 feet) in a matter of seconds.
- **Maneuverability:** Capable of high-G turns and abrupt changes in direction that would be impossible for known conventional aircraft.

### Sensor and Radar Evidence
The USS Princeton's AN/SPY-1 radar system detected multiple anomalous returns. These tracks were longitudinal and showed significant variations in speed and direction, often appearing and disappearing abruptly.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Cmdr. David Fravor:** Lead pilot of the F/A-18 intercept.
- **USS Nimitz (CVN-68):** The primary aircraft carrier involved in the CSG-11 mission.
- **USS Princeton (CG-59):** The Ticonderoga-class cruiser that provided critical radar tracking.
- **AAV (Anomalically Aerial Vehicles):** The term used to describe the unidentified objects.
- **Transmedium Flight:** The ability of the objects to move between the ocean and the atmosphere.
