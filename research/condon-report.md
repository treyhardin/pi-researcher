# The Condon Report (1968)

The **Condon Report**, officially titled *Scientific Study of Unidentified Flying Objects*, was the result of a large-scale scientific investigation into the nature of UFOs conducted by the **Condon Committee** between 1966 and 1968.

## Overview

The report was commissioned by the United States Air Force and led by physicist **Edward Condon**. The study aimed to determine if UFOs represented a threat to national security or indicated the presence of extraterrestrial technology.

## Key Findings

* **No Evidence of Extraterrestrial Origin:** The report concluded that there was no evidence that UFOs were of extraterrestrial's origin.
* **No Threat to National Security:** The committee found no evidence that the phenomena represented a threat to the United States.
* **Call for Cessation of Investigation:** The report's findings were used as justification to terminate **Project Blue Book**.

## Impact

The publication of the Condon Report had a significant impact on the UFO community and government policy, leading to a period of decreased official interest and funding for UAP research, often described as a "dark age" in the field.
