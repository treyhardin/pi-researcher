# The Igili (Entities)

## Summary
The "Igili" (often associated with the Mesopotamian **Igigi**) refers to a group of subordinate, divine entities in ancient Mesopotamian mythology. In the context of modern anomalous phenomena research, they are frequently interpreted as non-human intelligences (NHI), potentially interdimensional or extraterrestrial in nature, whose historical presence is documented through myths of their service to the more senior Anunnaki and their subsequent rebellion.

## Detailed Findings
The Igigi are central to the mythological landscape of ancient Mesopotamia, specifically within the *Atrahasis* and *Enuma Elish* traditions. They are described as the "younger gods" who were tasked with the heavy labor of maintaining the earth and its watercourses (e.g., digging the rivers Tigris and Euphrates).

Key aspects of their mythology include:
- **Subordination to Anunnaki:** The Igigi served the Anunnaki, a more senior group of deities.
- **The Great Rebellion:** Due to the exhaustion caused by their labor, the Igigi rose in revolt against the Anunic, leading to a crisis that eventually necessitated the creation of humans (the *Lullu*) to alleviate the workload of the gods.
- **Interpreted Nature:** In modern fringe and "ancient astronaut" theories, the Igigi are often re-contextualized as an advanced, perhaps inorganic or technological, race (sometimes linked to "Ancient AI") that interacted with early human civilizations.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Igigi:** The subordinate deities/entities.
- **Anunnaki:** The senior, ruling deities.
- **Atrahasis Epic:** The primary source text for the Igli rebellion.
- **Humanity's Origin:** The creation of humans was a direct consequence of the Igigi's strike/rebellion.
