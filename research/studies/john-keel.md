# John Keel

## Summary
John Alva Keel (1930–2009) was an American journalist and highly influential ufologist known for his work in investigating paranormal phenomena. He is most famous as the author of *The Mothman Prophecies*. Unlike many of his contemporaries who focused on the "nuts-and-bolts" extraterrestrial hypothesis, Keel proposed the **Ultraterrestrial Hypothesis**, suggesting that UFOs and related phenomena (such as poltergeists and cryptids) are manifestations of a non-human or spiritual intelligence that manipulates human perception.

## Detailed Findings
Keel's research was characterized by an incredibly broad scope and an intensive, almost obsessive, commitment to investigating a wide array of seemingly unrelated phenomena.

### Key Research Areas
* **UFOs and the Ultraterrestrial Hypothesis:** Keel moved away from the idea of aliens traveling from distant planets. Instead, he argued that these entities belong to a different dimension or "ultraterrestrial" realm, interacting with Earth in ways that defy conventional physics.
* **The Mothman:** His investigation into the sightings of the Mothman in Point Pleasant, West Virginia, became a cornerstone of modern paranormal lore and a primary subject of his most famous book.
* **Interconnectedness of Phenomena:** A central pillar of Keel's work was the idea that UFO sightings, cryptid appearances (like the Mothman), poltergeist activity, and even "Men in Black" encounters are all interconnected pieces of a single, larger mystery.
* **Fieldwork and Interviews:** Keel traveled extensively through the United States, conducting thousands of interviews with witnesses across various states, particularly in the Ohio River Valley. His work was deeply grounded in personal, first-hand accounts.

### Major Works
* ***UFOs: Operation Trojan Horse* (1970):** An early, foundational work detailing his investigations into UFOs.
* ***Strange Creatures from Time and Space* (1970):** Explores the overlap between UFOs and more terrestrial "monsters."
* ***Our Haunted Planet* (1971):** Further develops his theories on the pervasive nature of the paranormal.
* ***The Mothman Prophecies* (1980):** His most enduring and widely known work, detailing the Point Pleasant incidents.

## Key Entities/Facts
* **Alva John Keel (1930–2009):** The primary subject.
* **The Mothman:** The central figure in his most famous investigation.
* **Ultraterrestrial Hypothesis:** The core theoretical framework proposed by Keel.
* **Men In Black:** A phenomenon Keel helped popularize and investigate as part of the broader mystery.
* **Point Pleasant, West Virginia:** The primary location of the Mothman investigations.
