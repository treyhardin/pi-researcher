# NDAA Biological Evidence

## Summary
The National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) has recently included pivotal amendments and provisions aimed at the disclosure of Unidentified Anomalable Phenomena (UAP) and potential non-human intelligence (NHI) evidence. A significant focus has been on the acquisition and oversight of recovered technologies and biological evidence.

## Detailed Findings
Research into recent Senate amendments and House bills reveals a concerted bipartisan effort to mandate the disclosure of UAP-related information and materials.

### Eminent Domain and Recovered Materials
A key development was Senate Amendment 797 to S.2226 in the 118th Congress. This amendment proposed that the Federal Government exercise eminent domain over any and all "recovered technologies of unknown origin and biological evidence of non-human intelligence" held by private persons or entities. 

The implications of such a measure are profound:
- **Obligation for Private Entities:** Defense contractors, academic researchers, and private citizens would be legally required to turn over any discovered UAP-related materials or biological evidence to the government.
- **Impact on Commercial Interests:** Such a provision could significantly impact the proprietary interests of aerospace and defense companies that may have encountered or are researching advanced propulsion or biological technologies.

### Disclosure and Declassification Efforts
Bolid bipartisan groups of Senators, including Chuck Schumer (D-NY), Mike Rounds (R-SD), Kirsten Gillibrand (D-NY), and Marco Rubio (R-FL), have championed measures to accelerate the timeline for declassifying UAP-related records. These efforts are part of a broader movement to provide transparency regarding the government's knowledge of UAP and its potential biological implications.

### Legislative Context
The 2024 and 2025 NDAA cycles have been instrumental in bringing these topics into the legislative foreground. The focus is not only on declassifying existing records but also on establishing a legal framework for the reporting and management of newly discovered phenomena and evidence.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Senate Amendment 797 (S.2226/S.2026):** Proposed use of eminent domain for recovered UAP technologies and biological evidence.
- **Eminent Domain:** The principle that the government can take private property for public use, which in this context would apply to unknown technologies and biologicals.
- **Key Senators:** Chuck Schumer, Mike Rounds, Kirsten Gillibrand, Marco Rubio.
