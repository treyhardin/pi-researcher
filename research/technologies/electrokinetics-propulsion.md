# Electrokinetics Propulsion

## Summary
Electrokinetics propulsion (EK propulsion) refers to a class of propulsion technologies that utilize the movement of charged or polar particles within a fluid (gas or liquid) driven by electric fields. It spans a vast range of scales, from the micro-scale propulsion of robotic systems in aqueous solutions to nano-scale thrusters used for the precise orbital corrections of small satellites (CubeSats) in space. It is closely associated with the historical development of electrogravitics and the work of T. Townsend Brown.

## Detailed Findings

### Space Propulsion and Nano-Thrusters
In the context of modern space exploration, electrokinetic propulsion is a critical area for small satellite technology. Key aspects include:
- **Nano Electrokinetic Thrusters:** Specifically designed for low-thrust applications (micro-Newton to milli-Newton range). These are essential for the precise control and orbital maintenance of small satellites and CubeSats.
- **Electrokinetic (EK) Pumping:** The use of EK pumping allows for the precise metering of liquid propellants (monergolic or hypergolic) under purely electrical control. This is particularly useful for microthruster systems where mechanical pumps may be too complex or heavy.
- **Precision and Efficiency:** The primary advantage of these systems is their high level of controllability and efficiency at very small scales, enabling mission profiles that were previously difficult to achieve with traditional propulsion.

### Micro-robotics and Nanotechnology
The field extends into the realm of micro- and nano-robotics:
- **Microscopic Robot Propulsion:** Researchers are developing electronically integrated microscopic robots that use electrokinetic forces for movement and steering.
- **Self-Propulsion in Aqueous Solutions:** At the nanoscale, inhomogeneous surface kinetics can drive the self-propulsion of particles (e.g., bimetallic nano-rods) within liquids, a phenomenon useful in drug delivery and micro-scale biological sensing.

### Historical Connection to Electrogravitics
The term "electrokinetics" is often used interchangeably or in close relation to "electrogravitics" when discussing the historical work of T. Townsend Brown.
- **Brown's Legacy:** Brown's patents and research into the Biefeld-Brown effect often utilized the terminology of electrokinetics to describe the movement of ions and the resulting thrust.
- **Overlapping Concepts:** While electrokinetics in modern science focuses on fluid dynamics and micro-scale propulsion, in the context of UFO research and historical electrogravitics, it refers to the attempt to create a force dependent on an object's mass through electrical means.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Nano Electrokinetic Thrusters:** Low-thrust engines for small satellite orbital corrections.
- **EK Pumping:** Electrical control of propellant flow in microthrusters.
- **T. Townsend Brown:** Historical figure whose work linked electrokinetics to the concept of electrogravitics.
- **Micro-Newton range:** The scale of force typically associated with electrokinetic space propulsion.
