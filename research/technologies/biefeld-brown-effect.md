# Biefeld-Brown effect

## Summary
The Biefeld-Brown effect refers to an electrical phenomenon discovered in the 1920s by Thomas Townsend Brown and Paul Alfred Biefeld. It involves the generation of a net propulsive force (thrust) when high voltage is applied to the electrodes of an asymmetric capacitor. While originally interpreted by its discoverers as a form of "electrogravitics" or anti-gravity propulsion, modern scientific consensus identifies the phenomenon primarily as a result of electrohydrodynamics (EHD)—specifically, the movement of ions and "ionic wind" created by the ionization of surrounding air.

## Detailed Findings

### Mechanism and Physics
The effect is characterized by a force acting toward the thinner electrode of an asymmetric capacitor under high voltage.
- **Proposed Mechanisms**: Various theories have been explored, including ballistic ionic wind, ionic drift, and the impact of charge conduction between electrodes.
- **Scientific Debate**: There is significant debate regarding the physical basis. While some early researchers explored it as a gravitational or space-warp effect, recent studies (such as those published in AIAA journals) argue that much of the observed thrust is a misinterpretation of corona wind phenomena.
- **Vacuum Studies**: Some studies have investigated whether the effect persists in a vacuum to rule out air-based phenomena like corona wind, with findings suggesting the complexity of the interaction between electric fields and neutral particles.

### Historical Context
- **Discovery**: The effect was first observed in the 1920s by Thomas Townsend Brown and Paul Alfred Biefeld.
- **Electrogravitics**: The term "electrogravitics" was used by Brown to describe his belief that this effect could be used for gravity control and propulsion.
- **Research Era**: Research into this effect peaked during the 1950s and 1960s, contributing to the broader "electrogravitics" movement and the exploration of unconventional propulsion systems.

### Applications and Implications
- **Propulsion Technology**: Despite the controversy, the principle of using electrical fields to move mass (via ions) is foundational to modern ion propulsion and dielectric pumps.
- **Aerospace Interest**: The potential for low-energy, high-efficiency propulsion led to interest from both scientific and military-adjacent research sectors.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Discoverers**: Thomas Townsend Brown, Paul Alfred Biefeld.
- **Core Phenomenon**: Thrust in asymmetric capacitors under high voltage.
- **Key Terms**: Electrogravitics, Electrohydrodynamics (EHD), Asymmetric Capacitor, Ionic Wind, Corona Wind.
- **Primary Scientific Interpretation**: Electrohydrodynamics (EHD) / Ionic Wind.
