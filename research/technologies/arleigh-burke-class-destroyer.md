---# Arleigh Burke-class Destroyer (DDG 51)

## Summary
The Arleigh Burke-class (DDG 51) is a series of guided-missile destroyers used by the United States Navy. Designed to provide multi-mission offensive and defensive capabilities in air, surface, and subsurface environments, the class is a cornerstone of modern naval warfare. It features the Aegis Combat System, advanced radar technologies, and a versatile armament capable of conducting anti-air, anti-surface, and anti-submarine warfare.

## Key Features & Technology

### 1. Combat Systems & Radar
The ship's effectiveness is centered around the **Aegis Combat System**, which integrates sensors and weapons to engage multi-threat environments.
*   **Radar Systems (Evolutionary):**
    *   **Flight I, II, & IIA:** Utilizes the **AN/SPY-1D** 3D multi-function phased-array radar, providing continuous search and tracking of air and surface targets.
    *   **Flight III:** Features the advanced **AN/SPY-6(V)1** Air and Missile Defense Radar (AMDR), an Active Electronically Scanned Array (AESA) radar that significantly enhances performance in anti-air and ballistic missile defense.
*   **Additional Sensors:**
    *   **Surface Search:** AN/SPS-67(V)2 and AN/SPS-73(V)12 radars.
    *   **Fire Control:** AN/SPG-62 radar for missile guidance.
    *   **Electronic Warfare:** AN/SLQ-32(V)2 system for detection and countermeasures.
    *   **Sonar:** AN/SQS-53C sonar array and AN/SQR-19 tactical towed array sonar for subsurface threats.

### 2. Armament & Offensive Capabilities
The DDG 51 class possesses a powerful array of weaponry designed for multi-domain engagement.
*   **Vertical Launch System (VLS):** The heart of the ship's firepower, capable of launching a variety of missiles, including:
    *   **RIM-66 Standard Missile (SM-2)** for air defense.
    *   **RIM-162 ESSM (Evolved SeaSparrow Missile)** for short-range air defense.
    *   **BGM-109 Tomahawk** for long-range land-attack missions.
*   **Surface/Subsurface Warfare:**
    *   **Harpoon Missile Launchers:** For anti-ship engagements.
    *   **Torpedoes:** MK 46 or MK 54 lightweight torpedoes for anti-submarine warfare.
    *   **5-inch/54 caliber Mark 45 gun:** For surface and shore bombardment.

### 3. Propulsion & Hull Design
The class is built around a modern hull form, inheriting much of the machinery plant from the Spruance class.
*   **Propulsion:** Powered by four **General Electric LM 2500-30 gas turbines** driving two shafts, providing high speed (30+ knots) and responsiveness.
*   **Design Variants (Flights):**
    *   **Flight I & II:** The original design (DDG 51-78).
    *   **Flight IIA:** Enhanced capabilities, including increased VLS cells and advanced sensor integration.
    *   **Flight III:** The latest evolution, focused on the integration of the SPY-6 AESA radar and improved communications for modern multi-domain operations.

## Defensive Capabilities
*   **Anti-Air Warfare (AAW):** High-capacity missile defense and radar tracking.
*   **Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW):** Sophant sonar arrays and torpedo delivery.
*   **Electronic Defense:** Robust electronic warfare suites and decoy launching systems (e.g., Nulka).
