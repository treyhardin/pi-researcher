# AN/SPY-1 Radar

## Summary
The AN/SPY-1 is a multi-function, phased-array, 3D air search radar that serves as the primary radar for the Aegis Combat System. It is a key component of the Aegis Weapon System used on Ticonderoga-class cruisers and Arleigh Burke-class destroyers, as well as Aegis Ashore sites. The radar is capable of simultaneous search, track, and missile guidance functions for over 100 targets.

## Detailed Findings
- **Core Functionality:** The AN/SPY-1 is a powerful, multi-function, phased-array radar designed for automatic detection, tracking, and discrimination of air and surface targets. It provides search, track, and missile guidance functions simultaneously.
- **Technology:** It is a Passive Electronically Scanned Array (PESA) 3-dimensional radar system. It uses four complementary antennas to provide 360-degree coverage.
- **Versions and Upgrades:**
    - **SPY-1A:** Early version, installed on ships such as the USS Ticonderoga.
    - **SPY-1B:** An upgrade implemented on certain vessels.
    - **SPY-1D(V) "Victor":** A significant "littoral" upgrade designed to improve the detection and tracking of smaller, faster anti-ship cruise missiles in demanding littoral (coastal) environments with high clutter. It improved waveforms for better clutter rejection and increased transmitter average power.
- **Operational Role:** It is the primary air and surface radar for the Aegis Combat System. It supports tasks including ballistic missile defense, sea-based BMD, and air defense.
- **Manufacturer:** Primarily manufactured by Lockheed Martin.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Aegis Combat System:** The larger weapon/command-and-control system that relies on the AN/SPY-1 as its radar heart.
- **Lockheed Martin:** The manufacturer of the radar system.
- **Ticonderoga-class (CG-47) & Arleigh Burke-class (DDG-51):** The primary naval vessel classes utilizing this radar.
- **PESA (Passive Electronically Scanned Array):** The underlying radar technology used.
