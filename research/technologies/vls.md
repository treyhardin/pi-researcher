# Vertical Launch System (VLS)

## Summary
The Vertical Launching System (VLS), most notably the **Mk 41 VLS**, is a highly advanced, modular, and combat-proven missile launching system used primarily by the U.S. Navy and its allies. It consists of multiple cells installed below the ship's deck, each housing a missile canister. The system replaced older, single-purpose launchers (like the Mk 26) with a much more flexible and powerful architecture, capable of launching a variety of missiles including Standard Missile (SM) variants, Tomahawk cruise missiles, and anti-submarine rockets.

## Detailed Findings

### Core Functionality & Architecture
- **Modular Design:** The system uses a "cell" based architecture. Each cell can house different types of missiles, allowing a single ship to be configured for multiple mission roles (anti-air, anti-surface, anti-submarine) without changing the launcher itself.
- **Under-Deck Installation:** Unlike older rail or inclined launchers, the VLS is installed below the deck. This increases the ship's survivability by protecting the missiles from enemy fire and reduces the ship's radar cross-section.
- **Rapid-Fire Capability:** The system provides a rapid-fire launch capability, allowing the ship to engage multiple incoming threats (such swarms of anti-ship missiles) in quick succession.
- **Integration with Aegis:** The VLS is a critical component of the **Aegis Combat System**, providing the physical interface for the system's powerful radar and command-and-control elements to deliver ordnance.

### Key Advantages
- **Versatility:** The "plug-and-play" nature of the canisters allows for a diverse arsenal. A single ship can carry a mix of long-range air defense, land-attack cruise missiles, and anti-submarine munitions.
- **Enhanced Survivability:** By placing the launcher below deck and reducing its radar signature, the ship is harder to detect and the ammunition is better protected.
- **Improved Operational Availability:** Modular construction and standardized canisters simplify the replenishment and reloading process, reducing the time needed in port for rearming.
- **Combat Proven:** The system has undergone thousands of successful missile firings and has been deployed by 15 different navies on over 26 different ship classes.

### Key Components & Capabilities
- **Standard Missile (SM) Support:** Capable of launching SM-2, SM-3, and SM-6 for various layers of air and ballistic missile defense.
- **Tomahawk Integration:** Supports the use of long-range Tomahawk cruise missiles for precision land-attack missions.
- **Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW):** Capable of launching Vertical Launch Anti-Submarine Rockets (ASROC) and other ASW munitions.
- **Mk 41 VLS:** The industry standard, used on both Ticonderoga-class cruisers and Arleigh Burke-class destroyers.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Primary Manufacturer:** Lockheed Martin (and historically Martin Marietta).
- **Key Platform Classes:** Ticonderoga-class (CG 47), Arleigh Burke-class (DDG 51), and various allied destroyer/frigate classes.
- **Era of Deployment:** Heavily developed and deployed starting in the 1980s.
- **Core Technology:** Modular canister-based launch cells.
