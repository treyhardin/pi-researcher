---# Ticonderoga-class Cruiser

## Summary
The Ticonderoga-class of guided-missile cruisers is a class of warships for the United States Navy, serving as a key component of the fleet's high-end air and missile defense capabilities. Originally planned as destroyers, they were reclassified as cruisers due to their increased combat capability provided by the Aegis Combat System and the AN/SPY-1 radar. These ships are designed for multi-mission warfare, including anti-air, anti-submarine, and anti-surface warfare, and are capable of operating as flagships for battle groups.

## Detailed Findings
- **Class Overview:** First ordered and authorized in the 1978 fiscal year, the Ticonderoga-class cruisers (CG 47–73) were primarily built by Ingalls Shipbuilding and Bath Iron Works.
- **Combat Capabilities:** Primarily utilized in a "Battle Force" role, providing:
    - **Anti-Air Warfare (AAW)**
    - **Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW)**
    - **Naval Surface Fire Support (NSFS)**
    - **Surface Warfare (SUW)**
- **Aegis Combat System Integration:** A defining feature is the Aegis Combat System, which integrates electronic sensors and weapons. Key subsystems include:
    - **AN/SPY-1A/B Radar:** Passive electronically scanned array radar for detection and tracking.
    - **Command and Decision System (CDS):** The core computer-based management element.
    - **Weapon Control System (WCS):** Controls the ship's armament.
- **Propulsion and Machinery:**
    - **Engines:** Powered by four General Electric LM 2500 gas turbine engines.
    - **Power:** Provides approximately 80,000 to 86,000 shaft horsepower.
    - **Drive System:** Two shafts with controllable-reversible pitch (CRP) propellers.
    - **Performance:** Capable of speeds exceeding 30 knots, with an economical range of over 6,000 miles at 20 knots.
- **Ship Specifications:**
    - **Dimensions:** Approximately 567 feet in length and 55 feet in beam.
    - **Displacement:** Approximately 9,600 long tons (9,754 metric tons) at full load.
    - **Crew:** Approximately 330–340 personnel (including officers and enlisted).
- **Armament and Sensors:**
    - **VLS:** Equipped with the MK 41 Vertical Launching System (VLS) for launching Tomahawk cruise missiles and Standard missiles (SM-2MR/ER).
    - **Radar Systems:** Includes AN/SPY-1 radar, AN/SPS-49 Air Search Radar, and AN/SPS-55/73 Surface Search Radar.
    - **Sonar:** AN/SQQ-89(V) suite, including AN/SQS-52B/C/D hull-mounted sonar and AN/SQR-19 TACTAS/ITASS towed array sonar.
    - **Electronic Warfare:** SLQ-32(V)3 electronic warfare suite and AN/SLQ-25 Nixie anti-torpedo system.
    - **Other Armament:** MK 45 5-inch gun, CIWS, and various missile launchers (e.g., Mk-26 GMLS on earlier ships).

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Aegis Combat System:** The centralized, automated command-and-control system.
- **AN/SPY-1 Radar:** The primary radar-based sensor for the Aegis system.
- **MK 41 VLS:** The modular missile launching system.
- **Ingalls Shipbuilding & Bath Iron Works:** Primary builders of the class.
- **United States Navy:** The sole operator of the class.
