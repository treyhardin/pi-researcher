# Project Have Blue

The Lockheed Skunk Works technology demonstrator program that pioneered the use of faceted, radar-deflecting surfaces and radar-absorbent materials, directly leading to the development of the F-117 Nighthawk stealth fighter.

## Overview
**Project Have Blue** was a highly classified technology demonstrator program conducted by Lockheed's **Skunk Works** division in the 1970s. Its primary objective was to develop a proof-of-concept for a stealth aircraft capable of evading increasingly sophisticated integrated air defense systems (IADS) deployed by adversaries like the Soviet Union.

## Technological Breakthroughs: Radar Engineering vs. Aerodynamics
A fundamental shift in aircraft design occurred during Project Have Blue. For the first time, an aircraft's external shape was dictated by **radar engineering** (minimizing radar cross-section or RCS) rather than aerodynamic performance alone.

### 1. Faceted Design
The "Have Blue" aircraft featured a highly **faceted design**, composed of flat surfaces. This configuration was engineered to deflect radar waves away from the originating radar emitter, making the aircraft significantly harder to به detection.
- **Reflective Deflection:** The facets were designed to scatter electromagnetic waves in directions other than back toward the receiver.
- **Radar-Absorbent Materials (RAM):** While foundational stealth technologies existed, Project Have Blue utilized advanced RAM to absorb incident radar energy, further reducing the radar signature.

## Relationship to the F-117 Nighthawk
Project Have Blue served as the direct progenitor and technology demonstrator for the **F-117 Nighthawk**. The data and lessons learned from the "Have Blue" (and the "Hopeless Diamond" demonstrator) directly informed the design, testing, and eventual operational deployment of the F-117, which became the world's first operational combat stealth fighter.

## Historical Context and Development
- **DARPA Involvement:** The program was initiated as a response to a DARPA study revealing vulnerabilities in U.S. aircraft to advanced, networked, radar-guided surface-to-air missile (SAM) systems.
- **Competitive Selection:** In 1975, both Lockheed and Northrop were awarded contracts to compete for the program. Lockheed's design, developed by Skint Works, was ultimately selected.
- **Testing Environment:** Much of the critical testing was conducted in highly classified environments, such as **Groom Lake (Area 51)**, Nevada.

## Significance
Project Have Blue represents a watershed moment in aerospace history, marking the transition from conventional aeronautics to the era of **low-observable (LO) technology**. It proved that "stealth" was not merely an afterthought but a primary design driver that could successfully deceive sophisticated adversary radar networks.
