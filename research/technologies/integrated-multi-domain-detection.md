# Research Draft: Integrated Multi-Domain Detection Architectures
## Sensor Fusion for Advanced UAP Identification

### Overview
This research focuses on the development and implementation of **Multi-Domain Sensor Fusion** architectures to overcome the limitations of single-modality detection (e.	e., Radar-only or EO-only) in identifying UAPs exhibiting highly anomalous flight characteristics.

### Problem Statement
UAPs often exhibit **transmedium capabilities** and **low-observable (stealth) signatures** that can evade traditional radar or optical sensors. Detection requires a unified, real-time processing of heterogeneous data streams (Multi-Spectral, Multi-Domain).

### Primary Research Pillars

#### 1. Multi-Spectral & Multi-Domain Integration
- **Radar (RF):** Analyzing anomalous Doppler shifts and velocity-independent track persistence.
- **Electro-Optical/Infrared (EO/IR):** Detecting thermal anomalies and high-speed motion against complex backgrounds.
- **Electronic Warfare (EW) Integration:** Utilizing signal intelligence to identify non-standard EM emissions.
- **Reference:** `research/technologies/radar_data_research.md`

#### 2. Sensor Fusion Algorithms
- **Bayesian Inference & Machine Learning:** Utilizing AI-driven systems for real-time pattern recognition across sensor streams.
- **Edge Processing:** Implementing low-latency detection at the sensor level to reduce "signal noise" from sources like **Starlink**.
- **Reference:** `research/technologies/sensor-fusion-for-uap-detection.md`

#### 3. Detecting Anomalous Signatures
- **Non-Ballistic Trajectories:** Identification of extreme acceleration and abrupt vector changes.
- **Transmedium Transitions:** Monitoring objects transitioning between atmosphere and ocean.
- **Reference:** `research/technologies/non-electromagnetic-detection.md`
