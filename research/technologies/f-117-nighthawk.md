# F-117 Nighthawk

## Summary
The F-117 Nighthawk was the world's first operational stealth aircraft, developed by Lockheed's Skunk Works division to penetrate advanced Soviet air defense systems. Its unique faceted design and use of radar-absorbent materials significantly reduced its radar cross-section.

## Detailed Findings
- **Development**: Developed in the 1970s under the code name "Have Blue" and later "Senior Trend" to counter sophisticated Soviet SAMs.
- **Design**: Featured a unique angular, faceted shape to reflect radar waves away from the source, a departure from traditional aerodynamic designs.
    - Used radar-absorbent materials (RAM).
    - Incorporated inward-canted fins.
    - Non-circular tail to minimize infrared signature.
- **Role**: A precision-strike, subsonic, twin-engine attack aircraft.
- **Operational History**:
    - Proved highly successful in the 1991 Gulf War (Operation Desert Storm).
    - Participated in Operation Just Cause (Panama), Operation Allied Force (Kosovo), and Operation Iraqi Freedom.
    - Retired from active service in the US Air Force.
- **Key Features**:
    - Radar cross-section (RCS) of approximately 0.001 m².
    - Engines: Two General Electric F404 turbofan engines.
    - Payload: Precision-guided munitions (laser-guided).

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Lockheed Skunk Works**: The secretive division responsible for its development.
- **DARPA**: Provided the initial contract for the development of stealth technology.
- **Have Blue**: The prototype/testing program that proved the concept.
- **Senior Trend**: The subsequent highly classified development program.
