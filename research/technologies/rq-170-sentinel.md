---# RQ-170 Sentinel (Stealth UAS)

## Overview
The RQ-170 Sentinel is a highly classified, low-observable (stealth) unmanned aerial system (UAS) developed by Lockheed Martin’s Skunk Works division for the United States Air Force (USAF). It is designed to provide Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR) capabilities within highly contested and denied airspaces.

## Technical Characteristics
- **Design:** A tailless, flying-wing configuration, emphasizing low observability and radar cross-section (RCS) reduction.
- **Role:** High-altitude, long-endurance (HALE) ISR platform.
- **Capabilities:** Capable of capturing real-time imagery and transferring data via secure links to ground control stations (GCS).
- **Manufacturer:** Lockheed Martin Advanced Development Programs (Skunk Works).

## Notable Operations and Recent Activity
### Operation Absolute Resolve (2026)
Recent intelligence and media reports (early 2026) suggest the RQ-170 played an active role in **Operation Absolute Resolve**, an operation involving the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro and his wife. This highlights the aircraft's utilization in high-stakes, covert, and politically sensitive missions.

### Visibility and Surveillance
Despite its stealth nature, recent footage has captured the RQ-170 taxiing at Creech Air Force Base, Nevada, providing rare public glimpses of the aircraft's physical presence.

## Strategic Importance
The RQ-170 is a critical component of the United States' ability to penetrate advanced anti-access/area-denial (A2/AD) environments. Its ability to operate deep within contested territory makes it a cornerstone of modern electronic and aerial warfare strategies.
