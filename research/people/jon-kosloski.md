# Jon Kosloski

## Summary
Dr. Jon Kosloski is the Director of the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), the U.S. Department of Defense's (DoD) official office responsible for investigating Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP). He assumed this role in August 202
4 and has been a key figure in presenting AARO's findings and progress to Congress.

## Detailed Findings
Dr. Kosloski's leadership of AARO marks a significant period in the official investigation of UAP.

### Role at AARO
As Director of AARO, Kosloski oversees the investigation of UAP reports and the collection of data regarding anomalous phenomena. His role involves:
- **Congressional Testimony:** Providing regular updates and testimony to the Senate Armed Services Subcommittee on Emerging Threats and Capabilities regarding AARO's activities and advancements.
- **Data Collection & Retention:** Implementing improvements in how UAP data is collected and retained to ensure a more robust investigative capability.
- **Sensor Development:** Overseating efforts to bolster sensor development to better detect and track anomalous phenomena.
- **Reducing Stigma:** Working to reduce the stigma associated with reporting UAP events, encouraging more personnel to report sightings without fear of professional repercussions.

### 2024 Congressional Testimony
On November 19, 2024, Dr. Kosloski testified before the Senate Armed Services Subcommittee. Key points from his testimony and subsequent reports include:
- **Annual Report Release:** His testimony coincided with the release of AARO's annual report.
- **Mission Focus:** Emphas and the need for continued- congressional support for the office's mission to investigate UAP.
- **Partnership Building:** A priority for the upcoming year is building partnerships and promoting interoperability across the Department of Defense.
