---# Edward Condon

## Overview
Edward Uhler Condon (1902–1974) was a distinguished American nuclear physicist, a pioneer in quantum mechanics, and the director of the University of Colorado UFO Project, commonly known as the Condon Committee.

## Scientific Contributions
- **Quantum Mechanics:** A central figure in the development of quantum mechanics, known for the **Franck–Condon principle**.
- **Nuclear Physics:** Contributed to the Manhattan Project, specifically in the areas of radar and nuclear weapons development.
- **Atmospheric Science:** A leader in the field of atmospheric physics and the study of the Earth's atmosphere.

## The Condon Committee and the UFO Investigation
- **The Project:** Under contract with the United States Air Force (1966–1968), the University of Colorado UFO Project was tasked with a scientific study of unidentified flying objects.
- **The Condon Report (1968):** The final result of the study, titled *Scientific Study of Unidentified Flying Objects*, concluded that there was no evidence that UFOs represented a threat to national security and that further extensive study was unlikely to yield significant scientific advancements.
- **Impact on Project Blue Book:** The findings of the Condon Report were the primary driver behind the decision by the U.S. Air Force to terminate **[[programs/project-blue-book|Project Blue Book]]** in 1969.

## Controversy and Legacy
- **Scientific Scrutry vs. Dismissal:** The report was deeply controversial. Critics argued that the committee's conclusions were dismissive and that the study failed to address the potential significance of the phenomena, potentially stifning legitimate scientific inquiry for decades.
- **Historical Role:** While his work on UFOs is often viewed through the lens of controversy, his contributions to fundamental physics and atmospheric science remain monumental.
