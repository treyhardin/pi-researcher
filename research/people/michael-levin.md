---# Michael Levin

## Summary
Michael Levin is a prominent researcher in the fields of developmental bioelectricity, synthetic biology, and the study of collective intelligence. His work focuses on how cellular networks use bioelectric signaling to regulate pattern formation, morphogenesis, and the construction of complex living structures. He is particularly well-known for his pioneering work with **Xenobots**—synthetic, biological machines created from frog embryonic cells. His research explores the concept of "diverse intelligence" and how intelligence can emerge and operate across various biological scales, from single cells to multicellular organisms.

## Detailed Findings
Michael Levin's research at the intersection of biology and engineering explores the fundamental principles of life, specifically how information is used to control form and function.

### Developmental Bioelectricity
Levin investigates how endogenous bioelectric signaling networks, which involve voltage gradients across cell membranes, act as a mechanism for controlling growth and anatomical development. This field, known as developmental bioelectric signaling, provides a way to manipulate the "software" of development without changing the underlying "hardware" (the genome).

### Synthetic Living Machines (Xenobots)
A significant portion of his work involves the creation of **Xenobots**, which are programmable, motile, and multicellular agents derived from *Xenopus laevis* (African clawed frog) stem cells. These biological constructs can perform tasks such as:
- Collective locomotion (swarming).
- Self-assembly and reconfiguration.
- Carrying small objects.
- Exhibiting forms of "memory" through changes in their physical structure or signaling.

### Diverse and Collective Intelligence
Levin proposes a framework for understanding "diverse intelligence," which posits that intelligence is not limited to nervous systems but can exist in a wide range of biological systems. This includes:
- **Basal Cognition:** The ability of individual cells to sense, process, and respond to their environment.
- **Collective Intelligence:** How multicellularity and complex organisms emerge from the coordinated, intelligent activities of smaller sub-agents (cells).
- **The Physics of Sentience:** Exploring the physical and informational boundaries that allow matter to transition into sentient or cognitive entities.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Xenopus laevis:** The biological source material for Xenobots.
- **Bioelectricity:** The use of electrical signals for morphogenetic control.
- **Morphogenesis:** The biological process that causes an organism to develop its shape.
- **Collective Intelligence:** The intelligence emerging from groups of cells or organisms.
