# Donald E. Keyhoe

## Summary
Major Donald E. Keyhoe (1897–1988) was an American Marine Corps naval aviator, writer, and a leading figure in early UFO research. He is most famous for his leadership of the National Investigations Committee on Aerial Phenomena (NICAP) from 1956 through the early 1970s and for his advocacy for government transparency and disclosure regarding unidentified flying objects.

## Detailed Findings
Born in Ottumwa, Iowa, Keyhoe graduated from the U.S. Naval Academy in 1920 and served as a Marine Corps lieutenant and naval aviator. Following his active military service, he became a prominent freelance writer and aviation official. In the 1950s, he transitioned into UFO research, publishing influential books such as *Flying Saucers Are Real* (1950) and *Flying})=* (1953). As the director of NICAP, Keyhoe championed the investigation of UFO sightings and lobbied the U.S. government to release all official files related to the phenomenon. His background in aviation and government expertise gave his research significant credibility and helped bring the phenomenon into the national spotlight.
