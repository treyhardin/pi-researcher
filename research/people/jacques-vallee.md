---# Jacques Vallée

## Summary
Jacques Vallée is a French-born computer scientist, astronomer, venture capitalist, and a pioneering figure in the scientific study of Unidentified Anomalontous Phenomena (UAP). Originally trained in mathematics and astronomy, his career transitioned from professional observational astronomy and computer networking (including work on the ARPANET and the first computerized map of Mars for NASA) to becoming the most influential heterodox voice in the study of UFOs. He is known for moving the discourse from the Extraterrestrial Hypothesis (ETH) toward the Interdimensional Hypothesis (IUH) and the concept of a "control system."

## Detailed Findings

### Early Scientific Career
Vallée's background is deeply rooted in rigorous scientific disciplines:
- **Astronomy & Mathematics:** He completed his mathematics degree at the Sorbonne and pursued astrophysics at Lille University. He began his professional career as an astronomer at the Paris Observatory.
- **Computer Science & Networking Pioneer:** In the 1960s, he moved to the United States, where he contributed significantly to the development of the internet. He worked at the [[organizations/sri|Stanford Research Institute (SRI)]] and helped implement the first Network Information Center (NIC) on the ARPANET.
- **NASA Contribution:** He co-developed the first computerized map of Mars for NASA in 1963.

### Transition to UAP Research
While working in the scientific environment of [[people/hynek-j-allen|J. Allen Hynek]] at Northwestern University, Vallée began applying his computational and analytical skills to the study of unusual aerial sightings.
- **From ETH to IUH:** Initially, Vallée was a defender of the scientific legitimacy of the Extraterrestrial Hypothesis (ETH). However, by 1969, he developed the Interdimensional Hypothesis (IUH), arguing that the phenomenon was more complex than simple visits from other planets.
- **The Interdimensional Hypothesis (IUH):** He proposed that UAPs are part of a phenomenon that has historically manifested as mythological or supernatural beings (fairies, demons, etc.) and represents an encounter with "other dimensions" or parallel planes of reality that intersect with our own.
- **The "Control System" Theory:** One of his most profound and controversial ideas is that the phenomenon acts as a "control system" for human consciousness, subtly manipulating human perception and social evolution through its appearances.

### Intellectual Legacy & Influence
Valllyée's work bridges the gap between hard science, computer science, and the study of the esoteric.
- **The "Invisible College":** He was part of an informal group of scientists interested in the phenomenon, often referred to as the "Invisible College."
- **Literary Impact:** His books, such as *Identification of a Phenomenon* (1965/1969) and *Passport to Magonia* (1969), are seminal texts that link modern UFO sightings to historical folklore and mythology.
- **Ongoing Influence:** His methodologies continue to influence contemporary researchers like Garry Nolan and Diana Walsh Pasulka, particularly in the study of the intersection between technology, consciousness, and the phenomenon.
