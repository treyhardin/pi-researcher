---# Jay Stratton

## Summary
Jay Stratton is a retired high-ranking U.S. intelligence officer who played a pivotal role in the creation and leadership of three major government programs investigating Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP/UFOs): AAWSAP, AATIP, and the UAP Task Force (UAPTF). He is known for his engineering background and his leadership in bringing UAP investigations into a structured national security framework.

## Detailed Findings
A career intelligence officer with over 32 years of service, Stratton worked across various agencies, including the Office of Naval Intelligence (ONI), the Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA), and other sensitive national security sectors. His specific expertise in the UAP domain includes:

* **Program Leadership:** Stratton was instrumental in the transition and continuity between several key programs:
    * **AAWSAP (All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office/Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program precursor):** He helped transition the efforts of the DIA's AAWSAP into more formalized structures.
    * **AATIP (Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program):** Contributed to the intelligence-driven investigation of UAP.
    * **UAP Task Force (UAPTF):** He created, organized, and directed the Pentagon’s UAP Task Force, bringing a level of structured investigation to previously fragmented reports.
* **Investigation of the "Tic Tac" Incident:** Stratton personally conducted the first investigation into the highly publicized "Tic Tac" UFO encounter that occurred in 2004.
* **Operational Role:** Working within the Office of Naval Intelligence, he used his engineering and radar/electronic-warfare expertise to assess anomalous aerial phenomena. He was tasked by the Deputy Secretary of Defense to lead the UAPTF in 2019.
* **Public Disclosure:** While maintaining the discretion expected of a senior intelligence official, Stratton has participated in interviews (such as with the *Weaponized* podcast) to discuss the evolution of these programs and the importance of investigating these phenomena from a national security perspective.

## Key Entities/Facts
* **AAWSAP, AATIP, & UAPTF:** The three distinct programs/efforts Stratton helped lead or facilitate within the DoD.
* **Office of Naval Intelligence (ONI):** The agency where Stratton applied his expertise in radar and electronic warfare to UAP analysis.
* **"Tic Tac" Encounter:** A landmark 2004 UAP incident that Stratton personally investigated.
* **Engineering Background:** His specialized knowledge in radar and electronic-warfare systems was a cornerstone of his approach to UAP assessment.
