# Danny Sheehan

## Summary
American constitutional attorney, public interest lawyer, and educator who is a leading figure in the movement for UAP (Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena) disclosure. As a prominent advocate, he represents whistleblowers and pushes for legislative action to compel government transparency regarding UAP programs.

## Detailed Findings
*   **Legal Background:** A highly accomplished constitutional litigator with a career spanning five decades. His work includes representing parties in high-profile cases such as the Pentagon Papers, Watergate, and Iran-Contra.
*   **UAP Advocacy:**
    *   **Whistleblower Representation:** He represents various whistleblowers, most notably **[[people/david-grusch|David Grusch]]**, who testified before Congress about alleged secret UAP crash retrieval and reverse-engineering programs.
    *   **Legislative Efforts:** He has been heavily involved in advocating for the **UPE Disclosure Act of 2023** and the inclusion of UAP-related language in the **National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA)**. He has been a vocal critic of loopholes and ambiguous language in such legislation.
    *   **Criticism of Official Denials:** He has openly challenged officials like Dr. Sean Kirkpatrick (former AARO director) regarding the lack of transparency and the denial of evidence regarding UAP programs.
*   **Educational Background:** Harvard-trained (BA, JD, and Divinity School).

## Key Entities/Facts
*   **[[people/david-grusch|David Grusch]]:** Major whistleblower represented by Sheehan.
*   **UAP Disclosure Act of 2023:** Key legislation he has advocated for.
*   **National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA):** The major defense bill where he has fought for UAP language.
