---# Richard Hall

## Summary
Richard H. Hall (1930–2009) was a prominent American ufologist and a key figure in the National Investigations Committee on Aerial Phenomena (NICAP). Known as "the dean of ufology," he was recognized for his scientific and skeptical approach to investigating UFO sightings. He served as the assistant director of NICAP and was a major contributor to the organization's documentation of the phenomenon, most notably through his work "The UFO Evidence."

## Detailed Findings
- **Background:** Born December 25, 1930, in New Orleans (attended Tulane University). Died July 17, 2009.
- **Role in NICAP:** Served as Executive Secretary and eventually Assistant Director (approx. 1958–1969). Worked closely with Donald Keyhoe.
- **Major Works:**
    - *The UFO Evidence* (1964): Edited and published by NICAP, documenting over 750 cases.
    - *The UFO Evidence, Vol. II* (2000): Published by Scarecrow Press.
    - *Uninvited Guests* (1988).
- **Approach:** Emphasized a scientific, skeptical, and data-driven approach, often rejecting the more "fringe" aspects of ufology. He advocated for the extraterrestrial hypothesis (ETH) as a plausible explanation for significant sightings.
- **Legacy:** His work provided a foundational evidentiary record for the UFO phenomenon during the mid-20th century.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Donald Keyhoe:** Director of NICAP and close collaborator.
- **NICAP (National Investigations Committee on Aerial Phenomena):** The primary organization he worked with.
- **Colorado Condon Project:** Hall provided briefings and organizational help to the project team.
- **Extraterrestrial Hypothesis (ETH):** The primary framework he supported for explaining credible sightings.
