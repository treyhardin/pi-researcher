# Ryan Graves

## Summary
Ryan Graves is a former U.S. Navy F/A-18 pilot and the Executive Director of Americans for Safe Aerospace. He is a prominent witness to Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP), having reported sustained, near-daily encounters in U.S. airspace starting in 2014. He provided significant testimony to the U.S. House Oversight Subcommittee in July 2023, advocating for increased transparency and investigating the safety implications of UAPs for military and commercial aviation.

## Detailed Findings
Ryan Graves served for over a decade in the U.S. Navy, including deployments in Operation Enduring Freedom and Operation Inherent Resolve. As an F/A-18 pilot, he witnessed UAP encounters that were part of a pattern of activity in restricted U.S. airspace off the Atlantic coast. 

His testimony highlights that these encounters were not isolated incidents but a sustained pattern of unidentified objects performing highly advanced maneuvers that posed potential safety risks to naval aviation. He is a leading voice in the movement for better tracking and reporting of these phenomena to ensure the safety of both military and commercial pilots.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Role:** Executive Director, Americans for Safe Aerospace.
- **Military Service:** Former U.S. Navy F/A-18 Pilot (Lieutenant).
- **Primary Observation Period:** Approximately 2014–2016 (sustained pattern of encounters).
- **Key Witnessing Activity:** Near-daily encounters in U.S. airspace off the Atlantic coast.
- **Congressional Involvement:** Testified before the House Oversight Subcommittee on National Security, the Border, and Foreign Affairs on July 26, 2023.
