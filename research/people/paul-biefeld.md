# Paul Biefeld

## Summary
Dr. Paul Alfred Biefeld (1867–1943) was a prominent German-American electrical engineer, astronomer, and educator. He is most widely recognized in the context of unusual propulsion research due to his association with the Biefeld-Brown effect, an electrical phenomenon involving asymmetric capacitors.

## Detailed Findings
While Biefeld had a distinguished career in electrical engineering and astronomy, his name is inextricably linked to the discovery of the Biefeld-Brown effect alongside Thomas Townsend Brown.

### Biography and Career
- **Early Life and Education:** Born in Jöhstadt, Saxony, Germany, Biefeld moved to the United States in 1881. He achieved significant academic success, earning a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 1894 and a Ph.D. from the University of Zurich in 1900.
- **Professional Fields:** His expertise spanned electrical engineering, astronomy, and teaching, contributing to the scientific landscape of the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

### The Biefeld-Brown Effect
The "Brown-Biefeld effect" (or Biefeld-Brown effect) refers to an observed phenomenon where a high voltage applied to an asymmetric capacitor produces a net propulsive force toward the smaller electrode.
- **Discovery:** The effect was noticed by Thomas Townsend Brown in the 1920s, with Biefeld's name being associated with the foundational physics or experimental observations that helped characterize the phenomenon.
- **Scientific Debate:**
    - **Electrogravitics:** Early proponents, including Brown, believed the effect might be related to a manipulation of gravity (electrogravitics).
    - **Modern Interpretation:** Contemporary scientific analysis, including studies in vacuum chambers, suggests that the observed thrust is likely not an anti-gravity effect but rather a result of **ionic wind** and **electrohydrodynamics (EHD)**—the movement of air/gas ions being pushed by the electric field.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Paul Alfred Biefeld:** The person whose name is permanently joined with Brown's in the context of this propulsion research.
- **Asymmetric Capacitor:** The specific component used to demonstrate the effect.
- **Ionic Wind:** The scientifically accepted primary driver of the observed force in atmospheric conditions.
