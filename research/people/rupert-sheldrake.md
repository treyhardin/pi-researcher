# Rupert Sheldrake

## Overview
Rupert Sheldrake is a British biologist known for his controversial hypothesis of **morphic resonance** and **morphic fields**. His work explores the idea that nature possesses a form of memory, and that the "laws of nature" are more akin to biological habits that are passed down through generations.

## Core Theory: Morphic Resonance
Sheldrake proposes that self-organizing systems (from cells to entire species) inherit a memory from previous similar systems. This process, which he calls **morphic resonance**, suggests that the form and behavior of organisms are shaped by the collective history of their species.

### Morphic Fields
He posits the existence of **morphic fields**—organizing fields that guide the development (morphogenesis) of biological organisms. Unlike traditional genetics, which focuses on internal instructions, morphic fields suggest an external, formative influence.

## Connection to Paranormal and Psi Phenomena
Sheldrake applies his biological framework to explain "psychic" phenomena, viewing them as natural extensions of biology rather than supernatural occurrences:
- **Telepathy:** He views telepathy (especially in animals) as a form of biological communication mediated by morphic fields.
- **Brain as Receiver:** He proposes that the brain does not "produce" memories but rather "receives" or "tunes into" information from the morphic field, much like a radio receiver.
- **Formative Causation:** This concept implies that past events and behaviors influence current ones through the resonance of morphs, providing a framework for phenomena like precognition or collective unconsciousness.

## Scientific and Philosophical Impact
While his theories are considered controversial and outside the scientific mainstream, they have significant implications for:
- **Evolutionary Biology:** Challenging purely gene-centric models of evolution.
- **Cognitive Science:** Offering alternative models for memory and consciousness.
- **Parapsychology:** Providing a biological basis for investigating psi phenomena.
