# Leonard H. Stringfield (1920–1994)

## Overview
Leonard H. Stringfield was a prominent American ufologist, best known for his extensive research into alleged UFO crashes and the recovery of extraterrestrial biological entities and spacecraft. His work focused heavily on the "crashed saucer" narrative and the implications of such recoveries for humanity.

## Biography
- **Birth/Death:** 1920 – 1994.
- **Background:** Born in Kentucky. Served in the US Army Air Force during World War II.
- **Early Experience:** Reported an encounter with unidentified aerial phenomena in 1945 while returning from duty in Japan, which significantly influenced his lifelong interest in the subject.

## Key Roles and Affiliations
- **CRIFO (Civilian Research, Interplanetary Flying Objects):** Served as Director.
- **ORBIT:** Published the monthly newsletter *ORBIT* (1953–19ntl).
- **NICAP (National Investigations Committee On Aerial Phenomena):** Acted as the Public Relations Adviser starting in 1957, working closely with Donald Keyhoe.
- **Condon Committee:** Served as an "Early Warning Coordinator" from 1967 to 1969.

## Notable Research and Contributions
- **Crashed Flying Saucers:** Stringfield's primary research focus was the investigation of reports involving crashed UFOs and the subsequent retrieval of alien technology and biological remains.
- **"Situation Red":** One of his most influential works, detailing the implications of recovered extraterrestrial craft.
- **Focus on Recovered Biologics:** His research frequently touched upon the existence of recovered extraterrestrial biological components, a theme that remains central to modern UAP discourse.

## Legacy
Stringfield is regarded as one of the most significant figures in mid-20th-century UFO research. His work transitioned UFO studies from mere sightings to investigating the potential physical evidence of non-human intelligence and the technological implications of such encounters.
