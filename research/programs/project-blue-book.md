# Project Blue Book

## Summary
Project Blue Book was the official United States Air Force program for the systematic investigation of Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs). Active from March 1952 to December 17, 1969, the project was headquartered at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Ohio. The program aimed to determine whether UFO sightings represented a threat to national security and to study the nature of the phenomenon.

## Detailed Findings
Project Blue Book followed earlier investigations, including Project Sign (1947) and Project Grudge (1949). Throughout its twelve-year tenure, the project investigated approximately 12,618 sightings. A significant portion of these reports—701 cases—remained "unidentified" by the Air Force.

The project's termination in 1969 was prompted by the findings of a scientific study conducted by the University of Colorado. The study, along with other reviews, concluded that UFO sightings did not present a threat to national security, showed no evidence of advanced technology beyond modern science, and provided no proof of extraterrestrial origins.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Headquarters:** Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Ohio.
- **Timeline:** March 1952 – December 17, 1969.
- **Predecessors:** Project Sign, Project Grudge.
- **Key Figures:** Captain Edward J. Ruppelt (initial director), Dr. J. Allen Hynek (notable investigator/critic).
- **Key Statistics:** ~12,618 sightings investigated; 701 cases left unidentified.
- **Termination Basis:** University of Colorado "Scientific Study of Unidentified Flying Objects".

## Notable Cases
- **Nash-Fortenberry UFO Sighting:** Witnesses observed eight brightly lit, saucer-shaped objects flying in formation over the Chesapeake Bay.
- **The Incident at Exeter (1965):** A high-profile encounter in New Hampshire that gained national prominence and involved Dr\. Hynek.
- **Mariana UFO Incident (1950):** An early investigation involving witnesses in the Mariana area.
