# Project Sign

## Summary
Project Sign (also known as Project Saucer) was an official United States government study of unidentified flying objects (UFOs) conducted by the United States Air Force (USAF). Active for most of 1948, it served as the direct precursor to Project Grudge and was established in response to an explosion of reports regarding anomalous aerial phenomena over the United States following the 1947 flying disc sightings.

## Detailed Findings
The project was initiated by Air Force General Nathan Farragut Twining, head of the Air Technical Service Command. Initially named Project SAUCER, it was transitioned to Project Sign in January 1948. The primary mission was to collect, evaluate, and distribute information regarding unidentified aerial phenomena within the government.

One of the most significant and controversial aspects of Project Sign was its investigative conclusion. After seven months of investigation, the project's personnel—specifically within the intelligence division at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base—concluded that the phenomenon could potentially be explained by the existence of extraterrestrial spacecraft. This "extraterrestrial hypothesis" was highly controversial and led to significant internal friction within the Air Force.

The project's tenure was relatively short-lived, ending in mid-1949 when the Air Force shifted its policy. This shift, driven by a desire to downplay the issue and avoid public alarm, led to the replacement of the Project Sign staff with new personnel who operated on the premise that most reports could be explained through conventional means. This transition marked the beginning of Project Grudge.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Established:** January 1948 (as Project Sign).
- **Predecessor to:** Project Grudge.
- **Headquarters:** Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, Ohio.
- **Key Figure:** General Nathan Farragut Twining (established the project).
- **Primary Mission:** Collection, evaluation, and distribution of UFO information.
- **Controversial Finding:** Investigating teams explored the extraterrestrial hypothesis (ETH).
- **Fate:** Replaced by Project Grudge following a change in Air Force policy to downplay the phenomenon.
