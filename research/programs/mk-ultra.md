# Project MKUltra

## Summary
Project MKUltra was a large-scale, illegal human experimentation program undertaken by the U.S. Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) from approximately 1953 to 1973. The program's primary objective was to identify and develop drugs, procedures, and technologies that could be used for behavioral modification, interrogation, and mind control.

## Detailed Findings
MKUltra was an "umbrella project" that funded numerous subprojects involving various research areas, including pharmacology, psychology, and sociology. The program utilized a wide array of clandestine methods, including the administration of drugs (most notably LSD) to unsuspecting individuals, hypnosis, and sensory deprivation.

Key aspects of the program include:
- **Objective:** Development of techniques for altering human behavior and controlling the mind for intelligence operations.
- **手段 (Methods):** Used LSD, hypnosis, isolation, and various other techniques to study the effects of chemical and psychological stressors on the human brain.
- **Scope:** The program spanned decades and involved many academic institutions and medical facilities, often without the knowledge or consent of the subjects.
- **Investigation & Exposure:** The program came to light through investigations by the Church Committee and the Rockefeller Commission in the 1970s, leading to widespread condemnation and significant changes in intelligence oversight.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **CIA (Central Intelligence Agency):** The agency responsible for the program.
- **Office of Technical Service (MK):** The "MK" prefix refers to this office.
- **1953–1973:** The approximate timeframe of the program's operation.
- **Behavioral Modification:** The core focus of the experimentation.
- **Church Committee/Rockefeller Commission:** The investigative bodies that exposed the program.
