---# Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP)

## Summary
The Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP) was an unclassified but unpublicized investigatory effort funded by the United States government to study unidentified flying objects (UFOs) or unexplained aerial phenomena (UAP). The program was active from approximately 2007 to 2012, operating under the Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA).

## Detailed Findings
- **Origins and Funding**:
    - The program began in 2007 within the U.S. Defense Intelligence Agency.
    - It received approximately $22 million in funding over five years.
    - It was part of the broader Advanced Aerospace Weapons System Applications Program (AAWSAP).
- **Key Figure**: 
    - **Luis Elizondo**: A former Department of Defense intelligence officer who has claimed to have led the program and was instrumental in bringing public attention to the existence of UAP through the release of significant declassified footage and documents.
- **Significant Achievements and Impact**:
    - **Disclosure**: The program is widely credited with initiating the modern era of UAP disclosure. Its existence and findings were brought to the public eye in late 2017.
    - **Evidence Release**: The program’s work contributed to the release of high-profile Navy encounter videos (such as the "FLIR," "Gimbal," and "GoFast" videos).
    - **Congressional Influence**: The work of AATIP investigators and the subsequent public outcry helped influence congressional interest, leading to the establishment of subsequent programs like the UAP Task Force and eventually AARO.
- **Controversies and Challenges**:
    - **Discrepancies in Narratives**: There have been conflicting statements from the Pentagon regarding the program's specific focus (whether it was primarily about UFOs or broader aerospace threats) and the extent of Luis Elizondo's leadership role.
    - **Bureaucratic Hurdination**: Investigators faced significant bureaucratic obstacles and inconsistent responses from government agencies.
