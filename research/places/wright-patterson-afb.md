# Wright-Patterson Air Force Base (WPAFB)

## Overview
Wright-Patterson Air Force Base (WPAFB), located near Dayton, Ohio, holds a prominent place in the history of Unidentified Flying Object (UFO) investigations. It served as the headquarters for several United States Air Force (USAF) programs tasked with studying UFO phenomena, most notably **Project Blue Book**.

## Key UFO Programs at WPAFB

### Project Sign
Established in January 1948, Project Sign was an early intelligence-led effort to investigate reports of "flying disks" following the 1947 wave of sightings. It was a predecessor to later, more formal investigation projects.

### Project Grudge
A follow-up to Project Sign, Project Grudge continued the investigation into unidentified aerial phenomena.

### Project Blue Book (1952–1969)
Project Blue Book was the most famous and longest-running official USAF investigation into UFOs.
- **Scope:** It reviewed approximately 12,618 sightings.
- **Findings:** 701 of these reports remained "unidentified" by the end of the project.
- **Key Figure:** **Captain Edward J. Ruppelt** was a pivotal figure who led the project during its most active period.
- **Termination:** The project was officially terminated on December 17, 1969, after an evaluation concluded that UFOs did not represent a threat to national security.

## The "Hangar 18" Legend
WPAFB is central to one of the most enduring myths in Ufology: the existence of **Hangar 18**. According to popular legend, a secret facility known as Hangar 18 at Wright-Patterson is used by the U.S. government to store crashed extraterrestrial spacecraft and biological remains (often linked to the Roswell incident). While there is no official evidence to support these claims, the legend has become a staple of American pop culture.

## Historical Significance
Beyond UFO lore, WPAFB is a major military installation essential to Air Force logistics, research, and intelligence. Its role in aeronautics technology and its involvement in early Cold War-era intelligence gathering contributed to its reputation as a site of significant, and potentially secretive, importance.
