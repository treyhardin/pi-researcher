# Sea of Marmara

## Summary
The Sea of Marmara, particularly the area near Kumburgaz, Turkey, is a significant geographic location for Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) research due to the extensive video documentation captured between 2007 and 2009. The footage, recorded by Murat Yalcin Yalman, depicts metallic, disc-shaped, and oval-shaped objects appearing over the water.

## Detailed Findings
The Sea of Marmara serves as the primary setting for the "Kumburgaz UFO" case. Between 2007 and 2009, a series of over 25 videos were recorded by a night security guard, Murat Yalcin Yalman, near the Kumburgaz coast. The objects captured in the footage are described as being metallic in appearance, with shapes ranging from discs to ovals. Witnesses and researchers have noted that these objects appeared at sunrise or during the night, sometimes accompanied by red and orange lights. The visibility of these objects over the water provided a unique platform for long-duration, repeatable observations, making the region a focal point for UAP study in Turkey and globally.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Primary Location:** Coastal area of Kumburglag/Kumburgaz, Turkey, overlooking the Sea of Marmara.
- **Timeframe of Significance:** 2007–2009.
- **Type of Phenomena:** UAPs (disc-shaped, oval-shaped, metallic-looking objects).
- **Key Witness Connection:** Murat Yalcin Yalman (security guard).
- **Visual Characteristics:** Metallic appearance, sometimes accompanied by red/orange lights.
