# Al Taqaddum Air Base

## Summary
Al Taqaddum Air Base in Iraq was the site of a notable Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP) incident in October 2017. An infrared (IR) sensor on a force protection aerostat (blimp) captured footage of an unidentified object appearing to float above the ground. While the footage was widely circulated as a significant UAP encounter, the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) later assessed that the object was likely a cluster of partially and fully inflated balloons.

## Detailed Findings

### The 2017 Incident
- **Date:** October 23, 2017.
- **Mechanism of Detection:** An infrared (IR) sensor mounted on an aerostat force protection dirigible (blimp) operating at an altitude of approximately 2,700 feet.
- **The Footage:** The recording lasted 17 minutes and 30 seconds, showing an object that appeared to be stationary or slowly drifting above the air base. The footage is sometimes referred to in the community as the "Jellyfish" video due to the appearance of the object.

### Official Assessment (AARO)
The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), the Department of Defense's office for investigating UAP, conducted a formal assessment:
- **Conclusion:** AARO assessed with "high confidence" that the object was not anomalous in terms of capability or behavior.
- **Identification:** The object was identified as a cluster of fully and partially inflated balloons.
- **Methodology:** The assessment was based on full-motion video analysis and pixel examination techniques, concluding that the appearance was consistent with known balloon clusters observed in similar contexts.

### Significance in UAP Research
Despite the official conclusion of it being a balloon cluster, the Al Taqaddum footage remains a significant piece of UAP imagery in the public domain. It is frequently cited in discussions regarding:
- **Sensor Capabilities:** Demonstrating the ability of even specialized surveillance equipment (like aerostat IR sensors) to capture UAP-like phenomena.
- **Case Resolution Transparency:** The release and subsequent resolution of the case by AARO represent an important step in the government's effort to provide clarity on investigated incidents.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Al Taqaddum Air Base:** The location of the incident in Iraq.
- **AARO (All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office):** The U.S. agency responsible for the official assessment and resolution of the case.
- **Aerostat/Dirigible:** The platform used for the infrared detection.
- **Balloon Cluster Hypothesis:** The official identification of the object.
- **October 23, 2017:** The date of the recorded encounter.
