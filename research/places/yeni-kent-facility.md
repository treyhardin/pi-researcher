# Yeni Kent Facility

## Summary
The Yeni Kent facility, specifically the Yeni Kent Compound in Kumburgaz, Turkey, is the location where security guard Murat Yalcin Yalman recorded a series of significant unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) videos between 2007 and 2009.

## Detailed Findings
The Yeni Kent facility serves as the primary vantage point for one of the most famous collections of UAP video evidence in recent years.

### Context of the Sightings
In the coastal town of Kumburgaz, overlooking the Sea of Marmara, a night security guard named Yalcin Yalman was on duty at the Yeni Kent Complex. Using a camera equipped with a telephoto adapter, Yalman documented numerous objects appearing over the sea.

### The Footage
The recorded videos, spanning several years (2007-2009), display objects with the following characteristics:
- **Shape:** Often described as disk-shaped, oval, or metallic in appearance.
- **Behavior:** The objects were observed exhibiting anomalous flight patterns, appearing to float or change altitude/direction in ways that challenged conventional explanations.
- **Visuals:** Some videos captured objects at sunrise, while others were filmed during the day. Some footage even included instances of red and orange lights appearing on the objects.

### Significance
The "Kumburgaz UFO Videos" are highly regarded in the UAP community due to the continuous nature of the recordings and the relatively clear, close-up views of the anomalous objects. The footage has been analyzed by various UFO researchers and organizations, such as the National UFO Center and UAPedia, as evidence of potentially non-human or advanced technological aerial phenomena.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Location:** Kumburgaz, Turkey (overlooking the Sea of Marmara).
- **Primary Witness:** Murat Yalcin Yalman (Security Guard).
- **Timeline:** 2007–2009.
- **Facility Name:** Yeni Kent Compound / Complex.
