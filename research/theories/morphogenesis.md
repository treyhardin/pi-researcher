# Morphogenesis

## Overview
Morphogenesis is the biological process that causes an organism to develop its shape. It is a complex orchestration of cell division, cell migration, cell differentiation, and apoptosis (programmed cell death) that leads to the formation of complex biological structures.

## Core Biological Mechanisms
- **Genetic Regulation:** DNA encodes the instructions for cellular identity and developmental pathways.
- **Pattern Formation:** The spatial arrangement of cells into specific patterns and structures.
- **Morphogen Gradients:** The secretion of signaling molecules (morphogens) that create concentration gradients. Cells interpret their position and fate based on the concentration of these molecules.
- **Cellular Processes:**
    - **Cell Division (Mitosis):** Increasing cell number.
    - **Cell Migration:** Moving cells to their functional locations.
    - **Cell Differentiation:** Cells becoming specialized (e.g., muscle, nerve, skin).
    - **Apoptosis:** Programmed cell death to sculpt tissues (e.g., removing webbing between fingers).
- **Physical/Mechanical Forces:** The role of tension, compression, and fluid dynamics in shaping tissues.
