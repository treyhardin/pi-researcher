# Non-local consciousness

## Summary
Non-local consciousness (NLC) refers to a form of consciousness that is not exclusively generated by neuronal activity and extends beyond the brain and body, transcending the traditional boundaries of space and time and the five senses.

## Detailed Findings
Research into non-local consciousness spans several theoretical frameworks:

- **Theory of Psychic Quanta (TPQ):** This model proposes that "psychic quanta" act as informational qubits within a non-local psychic field. These quanta are thought to encode conscious experience in a superposition of states, which then collapses through interaction with neural gamma synchronization (40-100 Hz) in the brain.
- **Generalized Quantum Theory (GQT):** This framework applies the algebraic formalism of quantum mechanics to biological and psychological phenomena, attempting to explain quantum-like behaviors in systems that are not traditionally viewed through the lens of quantum physics.
- **Topological Geometrodynamics (TGD):** From the TGD perspective, non-locality (both spatial and temporal) is an inherent feature of the theory, with significant implications for quantum biology, neuroscience, and the study of remote mental interactions.
- **Phenomenological Evidence:** Evidence often cited includes phenomena such as telepathy, clairvoyance, precognition, and psychokinesis, which some researchers argue demonstrate the non-local nature of consciousness.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **Psychic Quanta:** Proposed informational qubits in a non-local psychic field.
- **Neural Gamma Synchronization:** The mechanism (40-100 Hz) through which psychic quanta might interact with the brain.
- **Non-locality:** The ability of consciousness or information to exist beyond the physical boundaries of the individual's body and the immediate surroundings.
