# Theory of Psychic Quants (TPQ)

## Summary
The Theory of Psychic Quants (TPQ) proposes that consciousness originates from a universal, non-local psychic field. Within this field, "psychic quanta"—acting as informational qubits—interact with the brain's coherent systems through neural gamma synchronization (40–100 Hz) to generate individual consciousness. Rather than the brain producing consciousness through emergent biological complexity, it acts as a bidirectional biophysical interface that stabilizes and interacts with these non-local informational states.

## Detailed Findings

### 1. Core Theoretical Framework
- **Universal Non-local Psychic Field:** TPQ posits the existence of a fundamental, non-local field whose quantized excitations are the basis of consciousness.
- **Informational Qubits:** The field's excitations, termed "psychic quanta," function as informational qubits ($\text{|}\psi\text{ brackets}$) in the system.
- **Biophysical Interface:** The brain serves as the biological interface that stabilizes and processes these quantum-level interactions.

### 2. Neural Mechanisms
- **Neural Oscillations:** The synchrony of neural oscillations, specifically in the gamma frequency range (30-100 Hz), is hypothesized to be the mechanism that facilitates the coupling between the biological brain and the field.
- **Gamma Band Synchrony:** The importance of gamma-band synchrony is central to the theory, providing a temporal window for the interaction.

### 3. Implications
- **Consciousness as Fundamental:** This theory supports the view that consciousness (or the seeds of it) is a fundamental property of the universe.
- **Quantum Biology:** It provides a framework for investigating the role of quantum effects in biological processes within the brain.
