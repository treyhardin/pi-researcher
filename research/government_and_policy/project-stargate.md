# Project Stargate

## Summary
Project Stargate was a series of programs conducted by the United States intelligence community (including the CIA and DIA) to investigate the potential use of psychic phenomena, specifically "remote viewing," for intelligence collection. While the program's intelligence value was eventually deemed insufficient to justify continued standalone funding, its history is deeply intertwined with the study of anomalous phenomena and contemporary UAP research.

## Detailed Findings
Project Stargate was not a single program but an umbrella term for various initiatives with different code names, reflecting a multi-decade effort by the U.S. government to explore unconventional intelligence-gathering methods.

### Program Origins and Code Names
- **Multiple Initiatives**: The effort included various programs under different names, such as **SUN STREAK**, **GRILL FLAME**, and **CENTER LANE** (primarily overseen by the DIA and INSCOM), and **SCANATE** (specifically funded by the CIA starting in 1970).
- **Purpose**: The primary objectives were to assess the progress of foreign intelligence services in the field of psychic phenomena, contract for basic research into the phenomenon, and evaluate "Controlled Remote Viewing" (CRV) as a viable intelligence tool.

### Methodology: Controlled Remote Viewing (CRV)
- **Technique**: The program focused on "Controlled Remote Viewing," a structured method intended to reduce subjective bias and "target" specific geographical or intelligence-related information.
- **Operations**: Operational units utilized remote viewers to attempt to obtain information about "targets" (locations, objects, or events) through extra-sensorial perception.

### Key Figures and Institutions
- **SRI International**: Much of the fundamental research was conducted through contracts with the Stanford Research Institute (now SRI International).
- **Hal Puthoff and Russell Targ**: Prominent physicists at SRI who were pivotal in the development and implementation of the remote viewing research protocols.
- **Intelligence Oversight**: The program involved significant oversight from the CIA (Office of Scientific Intelligence) and the Department of Defense.

### Conclusion and Legacy
- **Evaluation and Termination**: In the mid-1990s, a formal evaluation (following a 1994 Congressional directive) concluded that while some aspects of the research showed promise, the intelligence value was not great enough to sustain a separate, large-scale program.
- **Connection to UAP Research**: The legacy of Project Stargate persists in modern UAP discourse. Scientists like Hal Puthoff, who were central to Stargate, have continued to be influential in examining the relationship between consciousness and UAP phenomena, including theories about "consciousness-driven control systems" for unconventional craft.

## Key Entities/Facts
- **SCANATE**: The CIA-funded component of the remote viewing efforts.
- **Controlled Remote Viewing (CRV)**: The structured methodology used during the program.
- **Hal Puthoff**: Key scientist at SRI instrumental in the program's development.
- **Intelligence Community (IC) Involvement**: Oversight by CIA, DIA, and INSCOM.
